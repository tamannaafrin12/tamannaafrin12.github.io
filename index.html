Research Profile

About

Experimental condensed-matter physicist with five years of experience in cryogenic measurement, device fabrication, and materials characterization of quantum and van der Waals magnetic systems. Core strength is separating intrinsic device physics from extrinsic measurement artifacts through controlled temperature-, field-, and angle-dependent experiments. Hands-on experience with PPMS and MPMS/SQUID platforms, lithography-based micro-/nanoscale device fabrication, contact patterning, signal acquisition, and Python-based data analysis. Author of peer-reviewed work in Nature Communications and Nano Letters, with additional publications in Materials Research Express and RSC Advances.

Technical Expertise

Core Competencies

Cryogenic Measurement

Quantum Design PPMS; MPMS/SQUID magnetometry; dilution-refrigeration-adjacent low-temperature transport; rotator-probe angular measurements; cryostat setup, calibration, and noise/signal troubleshooting.

Device Fabrication

Photo-/e-beam lithography; contact patterning and interface engineering; micro-/nanoscale device processing; yield and contact-quality troubleshooting.

Transport & Tunability

Field-, temperature-, and angle-dependent Hall/magnetotransport; analysis of parameter-tuned device response, symmetry evolution, and reproducible transport trends.

Materials Characterization

AFM; XRD; SEM/EDX; TEM/STEM; XPS — structural, surface, and compositional analysis of quantum and van der Waals magnetic systems.

Software & Data Analysis

Python; Jupyter notebooks; OriginPro; LaTeX; ImageJ — quantitative analysis and publication-quality figure preparation.

Communication & Collaboration

Peer-reviewed publications; APS conference talks; cross-team coordination between experimental and theoretical groups; teaching and technical mentoring.
